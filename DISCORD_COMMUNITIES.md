## Vibe Coding / AI Builder Communities

### 1. Vibe c0de
- **Invite**: https://discord.gg/SW8V44SgVm
- **Post in**: `#show-and-tell` threads (explicitly for "share what you're building")
- **Vibe**: Global community for vibe coders, tool tinkerers, and indie hackers. Has dedicated channels per tool (Bolt, Lovable, Replit) plus bi-weekly mastermind calls. Very friendly to AI-assisted builds — it's the whole premise.
- **Notes**: Good fit for m1nd because the community explicitly values "tool tinkerers." Frame as a devtool, not a product pitch. The Show & Tell format means structural questions ("what structural bugs does grep miss?") land well.

### 2. BridgeMind
- **Invite**: https://discord.gg/bridgemind
- **Post in**: `#share-your-agents` or similar (7,000+ active members)
- **Vibe**: Agentic coding community — "ship software at the speed of thought." 50,000+ YouTube subscribers, 23,000+ X followers. The audience is MCP-native: they use Claude Code, Cursor, Windsurf daily.
- **Notes**: Strongest overlap with m1nd's target user. MCP tools are first-class citizens here. Lead with the zero-token angle — this crowd cares deeply about not burning context.

## Indie Hacker / Bootstrapped Founder Communities

### 3. Indie Hackers (official)
- **Invite**: https://discord.com/invite/sSHq2rAz
- **Post in**: `#show-your-project` or `#introductions`
- **Vibe**: The original indie hacker community. Mix of SaaS founders, solo devs, and bootstrappers. Values substance over hype — read the room before posting. Contribution first, promotion second. The culture is skeptical of anything that sounds like a product launch.
- **Notes**: Post as a technical person sharing a tool, not as a founder promoting a product. The r/programming title ("8 bugs grep can never find") works here because it leads with the problem, not the solution.

### 4. Indie Hackers Lounge
- **Invite**: https://discord.com/invite/jJyHjfS52y
- **Post in**: `#show-your-project`
- **Vibe**: More casual variant of the IH community. Lower friction for sharing, slightly smaller signal-to-noise ratio, but still grounded in building real things.
- **Notes**: Good as a secondary post after the main IH server.

- **Notes**: Small = higher quality conversation. Don't blast a link; introduce yourself and the problem first.

### 6. Build Sell 30

## Posting Strategy Notes

**Order of posting** (rough priority):
1. BridgeMind — most direct technical fit (MCP-native audience)
2. Anthropic/Claude community — product fits exactly
3. Rust community — strongest technical credibility builder
4. Hugging Face — plasticity/hypothesis accuracy story
5. Indie Hackers (main) — broadest indie audience
6. Vibe c0de — builder energy, good for async traction
7. Others as secondary

**What NOT to do**:
- Don't post the same copy-paste message everywhere
- Don't lead with "I built a tool" — lead with the problem or question
- Don't describe m1nd as "revolutionary" or "game-changing"
- Don't post in communities where self-promotion isn't allowed without asking first
- Don't ignore the question angle — "has anyone done work on Hebbian plasticity for graph structures?" gets more engagement than "check out my tool"

**What works**:
- Lead with a structural bug example that's genuinely hard to find
- Ask a real technical question you'd value an answer to (plasticity calibration, hypothesis confidence)
- Share the benchmark numbers without commentary — let people react
- The "39 bugs, 8 invisible to grep" stat is your headline everywhere
